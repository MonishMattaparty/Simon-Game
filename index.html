How To Play Simon Game,
A Pefect Guide


Step 1 :

If you are playing in Desktop or Laptop, then press any key on your keyboard.

Or
If you are playing in Touch Enabled Device, then touch on the part that is saying "Press Any Key/Touch Here To Start!".

Step 2 :

After Step 1 is completed. The game automatically starts after 3 seconds (3000 milliseconds).

Step 3 :

The game starts randomly by flashing any of the colored button.

Step 4 :

You have to Click (if you are playing in Desktop or Laptop) or Touch (if you are playing in Touch Enabled Device) the flashed colored button.

Or
If you press wrong colored button, then the game will end.

Step 5 :

If you have successfully completed Step 4, then another colored button will flash.

Step 6 :

Remember the colored button of Step 4, first Click or Touch it, then after that Click or Touch the colored button of Step 5.

Step 7 :

Repeat Step 6.

Step 8 :

Until & Unless till your brain fails to remember the pattern, then you will definitely Click or Touch the wrong colored button, then the game will end.

Step 9 :

Feel free to restart the game as specified in Step 1 or leave the site as per your wish.

Simon is an electronic game of memory skill invented by Ralph H. Baer and Howard J. Morrison, working for toy design firm Marvin Glass and Associates, with software programming by Lenny Cope. The device creates a series of tones and lights and requires a user to repeat the sequence. If the user succeeds, the series becomes progressively longer and more complex. Once the user fails or the time limit runs out, the game is over. The original version was manufactured and distributed by Milton Bradley and later by Hasbro after it took over Milton Bradley. Much of the assembly language code was written by Charles Kapps, who taught computer science at Temple University and also wrote one of the first books on the theory of computer programming. Simon was launched in 1978 at Studio 54 in New York City and was an immediate success, becoming a pop culture symbol of the 1970s and 1980s.
